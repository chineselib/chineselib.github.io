Who We Are

Chinese Library for Children is a community-grown nonprofit initiative that supports children’s access to Chinese-language reading through shared resources and local collaboration.
We grow organically from real needs in the communities we serve, working alongside families, volunteers, and local partners to make bilingual reading part of everyday life.

Where We Started

  • A conversation within the community

    My kids don’t have enough books to read. Buying books isn’t always easy. Even otehrs are willing to exchange books with us, we don’t know what they have—or how to find a time that works for both of us.”

  • A simple, open experiment

    We began as an open, home-based lending shelf—freely accessible to Pittsburgh families. Books were borrowed, shared, and returned voluntarily, without registration or barriers.

  • A growing, visible need

    As conversations expanded and we observed reading spaces across different neighborhoods and public libraries, one thing became clear: the need for accessible Chinese-language reading was not isolated or temporary. It was a shared, ongoing need—one that continues to grow.

  • Our Approach

    We respond to where interest and participation already exist. When a community shows demand, we work with trusted local partners to host and share collections of high-quality children’s books. Over time, as participation grows, these shared efforts evolve into sustainable, community-supported reading spaces.

    • Our work is sustained by families, volunteers, and partners who believe reading grows best when it is shared.
